# Calculus – Limits

## Definition and properties
- lim_{x→c} f(x) = L means f(x) gets arbitrarily close to L as x → c.
- Limit laws: limit of sum, product, quotient (denominator ≠ 0) is sum, product, quotient of limits.
- lim_{x→c} f(x) exists iff left-hand and right-hand limits exist and are equal.

## Standard limits
- lim_{x→0} sin(x)/x = 1 (angle in radians).
- lim_{x→0} (e^x - 1)/x = 1.
- lim_{x→0} (1 + x)^(1/x) = e.
- lim_{n→∞} (1 + 1/n)^n = e.

## Indeterminate forms
- 0/0, ∞/∞: try simplification, L’Hôpital’s rule (differentiate numerator and denominator), or substitution.
- L’Hôpital: if lim f(x)/g(x) is 0/0 or ∞/∞ then lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x) when the latter exists.

## Common mistakes
- Using L’Hôpital when the limit is not 0/0 or ∞/∞.
- Forgetting to check both left and right limits for limits at a point.
- Using degree of numerator/denominator for rational functions: divide by highest power of x.
