# Units and Domain Constraints

## Algebra
- Denominators ≠ 0. Under √ (real context) argument ≥ 0. log argument > 0, base > 0 and ≠ 1.
- For word problems: lengths, areas, volumes > 0; often x ≥ 0 or 0 < x < L.

## Probability
- All probabilities between 0 and 1. Sum of probabilities over all outcomes = 1.
- Sample space must be well-defined and exhaustive.

## Calculus
- Limits: check both sides when approaching a point; check ±∞ behavior.
- Optimization: domain often 0 < x < K or x ≥ 0; compare critical points and boundaries.
- Physical quantities (time, distance, area) non-negative.

## Linear algebra
- Matrix dimensions must match for multiplication.
- Inverse exists only for square matrices with nonzero determinant.
- Solutions may be required in integers or within a range; verify after solving.

## Verifier checklist
- Units consistent (e.g. m/s, cm²).
- No division by zero; no √ of negative (in reals); no log of non-positive.
- Final answer lies in stated domain and satisfies all constraints.
