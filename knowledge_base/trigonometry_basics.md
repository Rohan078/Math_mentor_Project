# Trigonometry – Basics for Calculus and Algebra

## Right triangle
- sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse, cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan θ = opposite/adjacent.
- sin² θ + cos² θ = 1; 1 + tan² θ = sec² θ.

## Radians
- π radians = 180°. lim_{x→0} sin(x)/x = 1 only when x is in radians.

## Common angles (radians)
- 0, π/6, π/4, π/3, π/2 and corresponding sin, cos, tan values.

## Domain for calculus
- tan, sec undefined when cos θ = 0; cot, csc when sin θ = 0.
- When solving equations, consider periodicity: sin θ = k ⇒ θ = arcsin(k) + 2πn or π - arcsin(k) + 2πn.
